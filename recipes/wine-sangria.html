Wine sangria: the perfect drink to beat the heat!

But...what is sangria?

It is an alcoholic beverage made from wine and flavored with fruits and spices. It is typically served cold and has become popular in many countries, especially in Spain and Latin America.

The traditional sangria recipe includes red wine and sliced ​​fruits such as apples, pears and oranges, sugar and spices such as cinnamon and cloves. Some versions may also include brandy or cognac.

The word “sangría” comes from the Spanish term “sangre” (blood) and refers to the reddish appearance of the drink due to the red wine used in its preparation.

Ingredients

  • 1 bottle of young red wine (preferably malbec)
  • 1 tangerine or orange sliced.
  • 1 peach
  • 1 apple
  • The peel of a lemon
  • Cinnamon
  • Juice of 1 orange

Wine sangria in 5 easy steps

  1. Cut the orange into thin slices and place it in a large bowl.
  2. On the other hand, chop the peach and apple in very small portions and add it together with the orange.
  3. Cut a few large portions of the rind of one orange and also the ginger and cinnamon. Incorporate everything in the preparation.
  4. Add the sugar to taste and the orange juice. Add just a little water or soda and the red wine to the mix. Mix everything very well.
  5. Take the container to the fridge and let the mixture macerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. After the time, add ice to the container, mix well and serve.
